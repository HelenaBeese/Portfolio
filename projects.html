Posters

Wanting to be Cool, Having to be Funny

Comedy Night at Ivy House, Nunhead

July 2024

‘Wanting to be Cool, Having to be Funny’ is a comedy night created by comedian and actor Jack Shep. In July 2024, I was commissioned to design a poster for their sell-out event at The Ivy House, Nunhead.

Referencing an image taken at a previous ‘Wanting to be Cool’ (photography by Molly Boniface), I created an acrylic and pastel painting titled, ‘Yazz.’ In collaboration with Felix Davidson, this painting was developed into the final poster and used to promote the show on socials, ticket platforms, and as a print.

Pauline Heaven Film Fundraiser

Fundraiser Gig at Windmill, Brixton

September 2024

Filmmakers Darragh McCann and Matt Lulu commissioned me to develop a poster for an event at Windmill, Brixton; fundraising for their new film, ‘Pauline Heaven.’

I created the central painting — ‘Heaven/Way Out’ — in acrylic and pastel, using imagery from the screenplay to select a collage of references to work from. Felix Davidson designed the text for the finished poster, which was used to promote the music performances and DJ Sets on Instagram, Dice, and OUTSAVVY.

Writing

To be Honest, Tracey

'A (re)Search for Love in Art Theory'

Central Saint Martins (Dissertation), June 2023

‘To Be Honest, Tracey: A (re)Search for Love in Art Theory’ is a project centred around two BBC radio interviews featuring the artist Tracey Emin. I transcribed these interviews and selected fragments to combine with my own memories of Emin’s work to create a piece of writing called, ‘Tracey & I: Portrait of the Artist by a Millennium Baby.’

In the dissertation, I used this potential narrative as a starting point to explore the role of memory and testimony in art engagement. With Joan Didion, Moyra Davey, and Clarice Lispector as key references, ‘To Be Honest, Tracey’ is an experimental work advocating for multifaceted, non-hierarchal, love-led dialogues between art and ourselves.

AT+P Magazine

The Problem of ‘The Wet’

MRes Journal of Art Theory and Philosophy, July 2023

MRes Art: Theory and Philosophy is a course that explores the relationship between practice and research; contemporary art and theory. Produced for the Central Saint Martins Graduate Showcase 2023, AT&P presents a selection of experimental, short-form articles – a sample of MRes Art students' diverse interests and methodologies.

For this project, I facilitated collaboration between CSM students and faculty and led the publication's design, production, and editing. I contributed a piece of writing called, ‘The Problem of ‘The Wet’,’ condensing ideas from my broader research into article form. I also developed a cyanotype collage, using film negatives and flowers collected from the canal path, to feature as one of the cover images.
